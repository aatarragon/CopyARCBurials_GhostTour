Bvt. Maj. William James Temple (1842-1863) [Section 16 Lot 1]

17th U.S. Infantry (Regular Army)

The 17th US Infantry was engaged at the Siege of Yorktown, Va., and later participated in the Seven Days Battles, Harrison’s Landing, Groveton, Second Bull Run (Second Manassas, Va.), Antietam (Sharpsburg, Md.) Fredericksburg, the Mud March and Chancellorsville, Va., where Temple was killed by a bullet wound to the chest (shot through heart). . He was promoted posthumously to Brevet Major for gallant and meritorious service.

Temple was the cousin of authors William and Henry James, and brother of Mary “Minnie” Temple, the model for several of Henry James’ main characters in his novels. Major Temple is buried next to Minnie. His mother’s side of the family were well known for owning much of the salt mines in the Syracuse area.
