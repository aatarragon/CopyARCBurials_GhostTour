Gen. John Rathbone (1819-1901) [Section 60 Lot 34]

9th Brigade, NY Militia

Prior to the Civil War, John had built a foundry for the manufacture of stoves. Rathbone's stove works became Albany's largest and eventually the biggest in the world. A traveler in the 1860s reported that he had seen Rathbone stoves in Turkey, Greece and even in Egyptian towns along the Nile River!

In 1860 John Rathbone was commissioned a brigadier general, commanding the Ninth Brigade, New York State Militia. He was also commandant of the Albany Recruiting Depot and helped organize 35 New York State Regiments during the Civil War.

In 1873, John was appointed adjutant-general by Gov. John A. Dix to the rank of major-general, serving a term of 2 years. He was one of the founders of the Albany Orphan Asylum, and founded the Rathbone Library at the University of Rochester.
