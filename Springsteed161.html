Col. Edward A. Springsteed (1840-1864) [Section 62 Lot 97]

43rd NY Vol. Infantry; 7th NY Vol. Heavy Artillery

Springsteed was an assistant teller at the National Bank in Albany and a boarder at 26 Hawk Street in Albany when the Civil War began. The son of a physician, he was commissioned as a First Lieutenant with the 43rd NY Infantry. He was engaged at Lee's Mills, Williamsburg, Golden's Farm, Savage Station, White Oak Swamp, and Malvern Hill. Discharged from the 43rdn NY, he re-enlisted as a Captain first with the 113th NY Infantry, which was later converted to the 7th NY Heavy Artillery. He was engaged at Spotsylvania and Cold Harbor and was wounded and nearly captured at Petersburg. Springsteed was killed in action, August 25, 1864, at Reams Station, Va.
