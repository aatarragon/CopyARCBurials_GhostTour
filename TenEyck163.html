Major Jacob H. Ten Eyck (1833-1898) [Section 8 Lot 15]

3rd NY Vol. Infantry; 154th NY Vol. Infantry

Raised a company in the 3rd NY Infantry in 1861 and served as its Captain and later promoted to Major. Transferred to the 154th NY. When Ten Eyck arrived in the 154th New York's camp he was greeted with a petition signed by every officer and endorsed by the colonel recommending that he resign. The officers of the regiment were opposed to an outsider taking a position of command, feeling it was “destructive of harmonious action” within the ranks. Ten Eyck did resign, and after just eight days of service with the 154th he was gone, replaced by a man promoted from within the regiment.
