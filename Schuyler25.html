Myndert Schuyler (1672-1755) [Sec 49 Lot 1]

Member of the Colonial Assembly, 13th Mayor of Albany, Church Master, Indian Commissioner, Captain of the Militia, Alderman, merchant.

Myndert Schuyler was born on January 16, 1672 at the family homestead, Schuyler Flatts. He was one of eight children born to his parents David Pieterse Schuyler, (born in the Netherlands) and Catharina Verplanck, between 1659 and 1678. Both of his parents died during the Schenectady massacre of February 8/9,1690.

In December 1693, Schuyler married Rachel Cuyler (1674–1747), the daughter of Hendrick Cuyler and Anna Schempoes, pioneers of New Netherland, at the New York Reformed Church, New York City. Together, they were the parents of one child, a daughter Anna.

In business Myndert was a prominent merchant and trader who turned his profits into extensive real estate holdings throughout the New York region.

He served the Albany government as a juror, constable, Alderman, and mayor. He also was elected five times to represent Albany in the New York General Assembly, serving from 1701 to 1709, in 1713, from 1716 to 1726, and again in 1728. In addition, he was the Albany County surrogate for many years.

In 1726, Schuyler was appointed and began serving as the 13th Mayor of Albany for two non-consecutive times. The first term began in 1719 and he was in office until 1720 when he was succeeded by Pieter Van Brugh. Schuyler succeeded him in 1723 and was in office until 1725 when he was replaced by Johannes Cuyler. While he was Myndert was Mayor, the city constructed small houses outside the city walls to house the Indians that came to trade.

For thirty-five years, the longest of any Commissioner, he served as Commissioner of Indian Affairs for the Province of New York.

Schuyler died in October 18, 1755 and was buried beneath the Albany Dutch church, later reinterred to the Albany Rural Cemetery, Section 49.
