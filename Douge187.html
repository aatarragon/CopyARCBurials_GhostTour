Susan Douge (1805-1897), Michael Douge (1804-1883) [Section 99 Lot 3]

Wife and husband activly involved in abolition before the Civil War and equal rights after the War.

To learn more about Susan Douge's involvement with Woman’s Suffrage click link #1

1. More Info on Susan Douge & Womens Suffrage.

Michael Douge was born in New Yok City in 1804, son of a freeman. Susan (Ames) was born in Albany, and little is known about her early years.

Michael Douge (New York City) and Susan Ames (Albany) were married on April 25, 1827 . The ceremony was performed by Rev. John Chester of Albany’s Second Presbyterian Church. Their wedding announcement was published in the Freedom’s Journal, the first African American newspaper in America.

After marriage, Michael began work as a barber/hairdresser. Toward the 1830's Michael became activly involved in African American community affairs. He wrote letters which were published in anti-slavery newspapers, became a founder of the Philomethean Society,a Black literary association.

At the same time Susan was a founding member of the Female Lundy Society, dedicated to charitable works in the African community and anti-slavery activities.

Both Michael and Susan were active in establishing the African Methodist Episcopal Church in Albany.

Michael died on December 9, 1883. His wife Susan lived until an age of 92, passing on December 21, 1897.
