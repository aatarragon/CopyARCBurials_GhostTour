Johannes Abeel (1667 – 1711) [Section 49 Lot 1 Church-grounds]

Second Mayor of Albany, Alderman, Judge, Sheriff.

Johannes Abeel was born to Stoffel and Neeltie Abeel in 1667, 3 years after the British took possession of the lands formerly colonized by the Dutch. His father, a builder and trader, died when Johannes was thirteen. Using the experience gained from his father, he began to barter for furs with Native American hunters inhabiting the lands surrounding his Albany home. This entrepreneurial business brought him to both the remote areas of the State and the commercial districts of New York.

Johannes prospered, carrying trade to New York, eventually taking over the family home on Market Street; the heart of Albany’s business district. At the age of 27, he married Catharina Schuyler, the sixteen-year-old daughter of Albany’s most prominent Dutch Family.

By 1688, Johannes Abeel had entered public service. He was elected alderman 1691-93 in Albany's third ward. Then, in 1694, following the term of Peter Schuyler, he was appointed the second mayor of Albany serving only for a single year. He would again hold the office of mayor from 1709 to 1710. Between his mayoral terms he was elected to represent Albany in the New York General Assembly during 3 years between 1695 & 1702. Johannes was appointed judge of the Albany county court in 1702; (and concurrently deputy mayor of Albany). In 1705 he was one of the masters of the provincial chancery court. He continued political life until his death in 1711 at the age of 44.

Johannes was a mainstay of the Albany Reformed Church, as was his Father, serving as deacon and witness more to many Albany baptisms. His will filed in June 1710, left everything to his wife and their children.
