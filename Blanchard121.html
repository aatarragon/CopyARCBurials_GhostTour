Bvt. Gen. Justus Wardwell Blanchard (1811-1877) [Section 10 Lot 6]

3rd NY Vol. Infantry; 162nd NY Vol. Infantry

Before the Civil War, Blanchard worked as a milliner (hat maker) and hair worker. He was first engaged at the battle of Big Bethel, one of the earliest land battles of the American Civil War, which took place on the Virginia Peninsula, near Newport News, on June 10, 1861. Justus took part in the Red River expedition and the siege at Port Hudson, La. He became commanding officer of the 162nd NY Volunteer Infantry in 1864 upon the death of Bvt. Brig. Gen. Lewis Benedict. After the Civil War he became a military storekeeper.
