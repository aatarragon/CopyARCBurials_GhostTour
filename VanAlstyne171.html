Maj. Thomas Jefferson Van Alstyne (1827-1903) [Section 28 Lot 11]

Judge Advocate, Congressman, Mayor of Albany

Before the Civil War, Van Alstyne studied law in Albany, was admitted to the bar in 1849, and commenced practice in the city, establishing he own practice in 1850. During the Civil War he served as judge advocate with the rank of major. After the Civil War, Van Alstyne served as judge of Albany County from 1871-1882. He was elected as a Democrat to the Forty-eighth Congress (1883-1885). He served as Mayor of Albany from 1898 to 1900.
