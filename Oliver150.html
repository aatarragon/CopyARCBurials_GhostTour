Brig. Gen. Robert Shaw Oliver (1847-1935) [Section 60 Lot 34]

5th Massachusetts Vol. Cavalry (Colored)

Born in Boston, graduated from military academy in Ossining, NY. Commissioned 2nd Lieutenant with 5th Mass. Cavalry, promoted to Adjutant, and later served as aide on the staff of the Twenty-fifth Army Corps. After the Civil War, Gen. Oliver enlisted in the Regular Army, serving as a lieutenant in Texas and captain in the 8th U.S. Cavalry in California, Oregon and Arizona, fighting in many Indian campaigns. Married Marian Lucy Rathbone, daughter of Gen. John Finley Rathbone of Albany. Employed by the Rathbone, Sard & Co. stove manufacturers, and ultimately was made a partner in the firm. While in Albany he remained active in the National Guard as Brigadier General in the 3rd Brigade of the NYS National Guard. Appointed Inspector General by NYS Gov. Alonzo B. Cornell. Appointed Assistant Secretary of War by President Theodore Roosevelt and continued under President William Howard Taft, serving for 10 years. He is the great-great-grandfather of actor Oliver Platt.
