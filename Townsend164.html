Bvt. Gen. Frederick Townsend (1825-1897) [Section 45 Lot 3]

3rd NY Vol. Infantry; 9th US Infantry (Regular Army)

Acting Assistant-Provost Marshall of New York State. Born in Albany, graduated Union College, was admitted to the bar and established his own law practice in Albany (Townsend, Jackson & Strong). Always interested in military affairs, he was made captain in the Washington Continentals Militia unit. He was appointed Adjutant-General of the State of New York. In 1860 he was appointed the first Commander of the Albany Zouave Cadets with the rank of Captain. In 1861 he organized the 3rd New York Volunteer Infantry Regiment and commissioned colonel. President Lincoln appointed him a major of the 18th U.S. Infantry and was assigned to western theater. He was engaged at Lick Creek (Pea Ridge), the siege of Corinth. He also took part in the battle of Perryville (Chaplin Hill), Ky. and was engaged at Stone River, Tenn., where Townsend displayed great courage in battle. He received brevet promotion to lieutenant colonel, colonel, and brigadier general, all in the Regular Army. In May 1863, Townsend was detailed for duty at Albany, as Acting Assistant Provost Marshal General. Townsend had the honor of being selected for the third watch over President Lincoln’s coffin when it arrived in Albany. Elected by the Republican state convention in 1880 for the office of elector of the president and vice-president, he cast his vote for James A. Garfield and Chester A. Arthur for those offices.
