Sgt. Major James Edwin Cross (1840-1917) [Section 30 Lot 62]

Medal of Honor Recipient, 12th NY Vol. Infantry (Onondaga County Regiment)

Enlisted in April 1861, at Batavia and mustered in as private. Promoted to corporal and sergeant-major. He was awarded the Medal of Honor for most distinguished gallantry at Blackburn's Ford, Va. on July 18, 1861, during the First Bull Run campaign (First Manassas). His Medal of Honor citation reads in part, “with a companion refused to re-treat when all around him were driven back in disarray; held his ground and caused a counter-attack.” (Like many Civil War soldiers, his Medal of Honor wasn’t awarded until well after the War had ended, in April 1898.) Cross was later wounded at Gaines Mills, Va. in June 1862 and discharged for disability that September.
