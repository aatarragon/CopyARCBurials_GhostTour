1st Sergt. Samuel O. “One Armed” Berry (1839-1873) [Section 98 Tier 9, Grave 19]

6th Kentucky Vol. Cavalry, Confederate States of America

Born in Missouri. At some point his right arm had been severed below his elbow and afterward he was known as “One Armed” Berry. Served with 6th Kentucky Cavalry under Confederate General John Hunt Morgan, and later with guerilla units led by Henry C. Magruder, Bill Marion, William Quantrill, James Younger and Frank James. They terrorized the residents of the State of Kentucky during the Civil War, robbing, killing and raping many civilians. Berry alone was convicted of 12 counts of murder and 6 counts of robbery. His sentence to be hanged was commuted to 10 years at hard labor at the Albany Penitentiary, where he died of consumption after serving 7 years of his sentence. He was buried in Single Grave 19, Tier 9, Section 98, in a plot reserved for prisoners from the penitentiary.
