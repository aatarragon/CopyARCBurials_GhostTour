Col. George W. Pratt (1830-1862) [Section 44 Lot 3]

20th NYS Militia; 80th NY Vol. Infantry

Son of Zadock and Abagail Pratt of Prattsville. Family involved in tanning, banking and farming, Prior to the Civil War, George was a NYS Senator and Quartermaster General of NY.

Pratt was mortally wounded at Second Bull Run (Second Manassas) after being struck by a minie ball in the right shoulder, the ball lodging in his spinal column, paralyzing him from the waist down. He died of his wounds in Albany, September 11, 1862.
