Gen. John Dickerman (1833-1885) [Section 21 Lot 33]

First service held in the Albany Rural Cemetery Chapel

In 1877, Dickerman commanded Albany’s Ninth Brigade of Militia and later was an auctioneer and manager of Albany’s Music Hall on the corner of Pearl and Beaver Streets (“two performances a day”). He was a real estate agent and stockbroker located in Albany’s famous Tweddle Hall. He sold furniture and art and did appraisals. Described as genial, witty, popular and public spirited. “He fought death bravely and cheerfully ‘til the last.” “General,” said a friend to him one day, “you are not looking well; why don’t you go south for the winter?” “No,” was the reply with a sad smile, and pointing toward the Rural Cemetery, “I’m going north.” His service was the first service held in the Albany Rural Cemetery Chapel.
