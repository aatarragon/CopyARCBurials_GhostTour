Brig. Gen. John Henry Patterson (1843-1920) [Section 42 Lot 13]

Medal of Honor Recipient, 11th United States Infantry (Regular Army)

Born in Selkirk. Entered service in Maine. Served in the 1st Battalion of the 11th U.S. Infantry. Patterson was awarded the Medal of Honor for actions at the Wilderness, May 5, 1864. His Medal of Honor citation reads: “Under the heavy fire of the advancing enemy, picked up and carried several hundred yards to a place of safety a wounded officer of his regiment who was helpless and would otherwise have been burned in the forest.” He was appointed Brigadier General of Volunteers from September 21, 1898 to November 30, 1898.
