Col. Michael K. Bryan (1822-1863) [Section 43 Lot 4]

Worth Guards Militia Company; 25th NYS Militia; 175th NY Vol. Infantry

Born in Ireland, Michael came to Albany in 1827. He was employed for many years by his cousin, Col. John McArdle (buried in St. Agnes Cemetery, Menands). In 1853 Bryan became the proprietor of a restaurant and hotel. In 1850, Michael K. Bryan became a captain in the Worth Guards, one of the military companies in the City of Albany. Commanded 25th NYS Militia which served at Washington, DC where he led the second regiment to leave Washington and cross the Long Bridge into Arlington, Virginia. Built “Fort Albany” on Arlington Heights, current site of the Pentagon. Killed in action, June 14, 1863, before Port Hudson, La.
