Individual biographies of themed tours


Biographies assembled within various tour themes.

To view individual biographies, scroll this list taking note of the tour name and individual of interest. Next, use the mapping program's search function to locate the individuals burial location, and view the popup to confirm correct identity. Clicking the image retrieves their full biography.


African Americans

Thomas Elkins, Albany's Renaissance Man: Doctor, Dentist, Druggist, Inventor, Abolitionist, Community and Political Activist.

Phebe Hoag Jones, Suffragette, Abolitionist and Social Reformer.

Stephen Meyers, Leaders of the Underground Railroad, Abolitionist lobbyist, prominent publisher, President of New York State Suffrage Association, Trustee of Florence Farming and Lumber Association, Headwaiter at the Delavan House, namesake to Albany’s Myers Middle School.

Arabella Chapman Miller, First African American Student to graduate from Albany Free Academy (Albany High School).

James C. Matthews, First black law student at Albany Law School, First black male graduate from a law school in New York State, First elected black judge in the United States, Lawyer to the case which desegregated Albany public schools, Successfully lobbied NYS Governor Grover Cleveland on a bill to protect the rights of black teachers.

Dianna Mingo, The oldest person buried in the Albany Rural cemetery.

Samuel Schuyler, Owner of successful black company, Schuyler Towboat Company.

Sibbie, Last slave to the Schuyler family whose grave was mistaken for Civil War soldier.

Harriet Alfrata Chapman Thompson, Chief Stenographer of NYS Library Catalog Department under Melvil Dewey, author of a novel which has been rediscovered by African American and feminist literary circles, possibly first African American to graduate from Albany Business College.

William Topp, President of several anti-slavery conventions in New York State, member of Albany’s Underground Railroad, and women’s rights activist.

Rosanna Vosburgh, “Well respected servant”.

Catherine Mary Williams, African American Vice President of the Albany Women’s Suffrage Society, and teacher in Albany's segragated Wilberforce School and for the Freedman's Bureau in the South after the Civil War.

Charles Calverley, Sculptor and Stone Carver,

Samantha Littlefield Huntley, One of the first well known female painters,

Lathrop Family Plot, A family of successful female artists,

Erastus Dow Palmer, 19th century neoclassical sculptor, artist of Angel at the Sepulchre, pre-eminent conch shell artist,

Frances Starr, Actress of the stage, screen and early television,

Alice Morgan Wright, Modernist Sculptor, Co-founder of New York State League of Women Voters, Recording Secretary for New York State Suffrage Party, Aided in the French and British Suffragist Movement, co-founder of the National Woman’s Party, Founder of Humane Education Society, successfully lobbied for the U.S. Humane Slaughter Act of 1958,

Independence

Jan Jansen Bleeker, Member of the Provincial Assembly, 7th Mayor of Albany, Indian Commissioner, Captain of the Militia, Justice of the Peace.

Hendrick Hansen, 5th Mayor of Albany, Provincial Assemblyman, Alderman, Indian Com-missioner, trader, merchant.

Philip Schuyler, Colonel in the French-Indian War, Member of the Continental Congress, Major General in the Revolutionary War, First of two US Senators from NY, NY Assembly, NY State Senator, built the first flax-mill in the United States, ran a lumber and timber operation from his Schuylerville home.

Veteran of the Revolutionary War.

Abraham Ten Broeck, Member of Colonial Assembly, Member of the Provincial Congress, Delegate to the Continental Congress at Philadelphia, General in the Colonial Militia, 28th Mayor of Albany, State Senator, Justice of the Court of Common Pleas, President of the Bank of Albany, Merchant, built the Ten Broeck Mansion.

Jacob Coenraedt Ten Eyck, 22nd Mayor of Albany, Indian Commissioner, silversmith, judge, Member of Albany’s Committee of Safety during the Revolutionary War.

Solomon Van Rensselaer, Adjutant General of the New York State militia.

Robert Yates, Revolutionary Patriot, Member of Albany’s Committee of Safety. Supervised the construction of the “Chain across the Hudson River, received a message from Ethan Allen on 5/12/1776 notifying them that he had captured Ticonderoga, Member of the NY Provincial Congress – received a copy of the Declaration of Independence and sent it on to Albany. Member of the Committee that drafted NY’s first constitution, Chief Judge of the NY State Supreme Court.

Christopher A Yates, Revolutionary war Lieutenant, Dutch Church Pewholder, Albany third ward Constable.
