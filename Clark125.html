Lt. Harrison Clark (1842-1913) [Section 112 Lot 153]

Medal of Honor Recipient, 125th NY Vol. Infantry

Clark was born in 1842 in Chatham. A wagon maker by trade, in 1862 he enlisted in the 125th NY Infantry and mustered in as a corporal. Clark was awarded the Medal of Honor for heroism at the Battle of Gettysburg on July 2, 1863, when the color bearer fell wounded. Clark dropped his weapon, picked up the flag, and led the regiment toward the Confederate line. The Confederate advance was stopped. Clark was promoted to Color Sergeant on the field the following day. His Medal of Honor citation reads as follows: “seized the flag from a fallen soldier and although shot in the leg, led a charge to repel an attack.” Ten months later at the Battle of the Wilderness, Clark carried the flag to within 10 feet of the enemy’s line, where he was wounded, his left thigh shattered by a minie ball. Clark was promoted to lieutenant, but his left leg was amputated and he was mustered out of the army.
