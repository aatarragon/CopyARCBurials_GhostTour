Peter Pedrom Schuyler Jr, (1698-1779) [Section 49]

Peter was the son of Peter Philip Schuyler, the first mayor of Albany, and Catherine Groesbech Schuyler.

Peter Schuyler lived his entire life near the Rensselaerwyck Mansion in an area known as "The Flats." He inherited Winter's Plantation as part of his mother's inheritance. Peter singularly of his father's sons continued their lineage of the Schuyler family.

As a young man Peter Schuyler was bold and fearless. Under the direction of New York Governor Burnet, he was commissioned a captain and was given the order to erect and defend frontier Fort Irondequoit, an outpost on Lake Ontario in what is now Monroe County. Accompanied by ten men, Peter accomplished this mission, working under constant danger and hardship. In his later years he became deaf and was confined to the family farms.
