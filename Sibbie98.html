Sibbie Schuyler (? – 1862) [Section 98 Tier 1 Grave 1]

Last slave to the Schuyler family whose grave was mistaken for Civil War soldier

In 2016, Albany Rural Cemetery Historian, Paula Lemire came across a burial index card for a woman identified as "Libbie," a servant of the Schuyler family. At first glance, it seemed this might be the same Libby who is recorded as being a slave at the Schuyler Mansion in Albany as well as the family's farm at Saratoga. The search for her headstone within the cemetery uncovered a different story.

In recent years, there has been much attention given to slaves of the Schuyler family’s residence at “The Flatts”. In 2005, excavations at a commercial site across Broadway (Route 32) from the Schuyler Flatts Park uncovered the bones of slaves buried in a plot separate from the Schuyler family's own burial ground. After a period of examination by specialists at the New York State Museum, these remains were reburied on June 17, 2016, at the Saint Agnes Roman Catholic Cemetery near their original resting place. The names of these individuals remain unknown; their life stories constructed as possible from their bones and the circumstances of their original burial.

In the Albany Rural Cemetery is the long-forgotten grave of a woman who was the last known slave of the Schuylers’ at The Flatts. Located in the cemetery’s North Ridge locality, the tilting headstone is small and almost completely illegible. The white marble has eroded to the point where its inscription is only visible through rubbings. At one time, a flag and Grand Army of The Republic marker was placed next to the grave which indicated it was a burial place for a Civil War soldier. There are several Union veterans and one Confederate buried in the same section, and this simple grave is from the same period.

Recorded in the Cemetery's “Single Grave Book” identifies the grave as that of “Libbie Schuyler. (Colored). Widow Schuyler's servant.”The front of the headstone reads, “A Faithful Servant of The Schuyler Family Died Nov 24, 1862,”. On the headstones curved top edge is carved the name, LIBBIE. A stamp on the page notes that “this record was made from the lot,” meaning it was transcribed from the headstone. However, when it was transcribed, a small but significant mistake was made. What appears to be an “L” is, in fact, an “S." The woman buried here is Sibbie, also known as Sibina or Sibby, the last documented slave at The Flatts.
