Gen. Dr. Samuel Oakley Vanderpoel (1824-1886) [Section 9 Lot 1]

Surgeon General of the State of New York

Vanderpoel completed his preparatory medical training at Kinderhook Academy, then entered the University of New York. He studied medicine with his father and graduated from Jefferson Medical College, Philadelphia. In 1857 he was appointed surgeon-general of New York State, and three years later he was chosen president of the Albany County Medical Society. In 1861 he was again appointed surgeon-general. The Civil War began, imposing duties and responsibilities far more arduous, delicate and important than had ever before devolved upon that position. His successful administration of his duties constitutes an important chapter in the association of New York with the Civil War.
