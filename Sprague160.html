Brig. Gen. John Titcomb Sprague (1810-1878) [Section 13 Lot 25]

Adjutant General of NYS; Lieutenant US Marine Corps; Colonel US Army

In 1834 Sprague became 2nd lieutenant in the U.S. Marine Corps, and served in the Florida war against the Seminole Indians, being twice promoted for meritorious conduct, and brevetted captain on March 15, 1842. He was brevetted major on 30 May 1848 and was made major of the 1st U.S. Infantry in May 1861. When stationed with his regiment in Texas, he was taken prisoner by Confederate General David E. Twiggs, but was released on parole. He was sent to Albany, NY to become mustering and disbursing officer, and Adjutant General of the state, with the rank of brigadier general, holding this post until 1865. He was appointed lieutenant colonel of the 11th U.S. Infantry in March, 1863, and colonel of the 7th U.S. Infantry in June 1865. He was made military governor of Florida and head of the Freedman’s Bureau from 1866 to 1868. Sprague retired from the army in July 1870.
