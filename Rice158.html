Brig. Gen. James Clay Rice (1828-1864) [Section 42 Lot 11]

39th NY Vol. Infantry; 44th NY Vol. Infantry

Rice was mustered in as Lt. Col. in 39th NY Infantry, where he quelled a formidable mutiny after Second Bull Run (Manassas, Va.) He was transferred to Albany to form the 44th NY Infantry and was soon promoted to colonel. With the 44th NY, he was engaged during the battle of Gettysburg, where he was one of the heroes of the battle for Little Round Top. Rice was promoted to Brigadier General in August 1863 and led a brigade during the Mine Run Campaign and the Wilderness. Rice was mortally wounded in May 1864 at Spottsvania, Va., shot in the leg. His leg was amputated but he died the following day. His last words while dying are engraved on the base of his monument: “Turn Me Over, Let Me Die With My Face to the Foe.”
