Lt. William Henry Pohlman (1842-1863) [Section 6 Lot 2]

1st New Jersey Vol. Infantry; U.S. Signal Corps; 59th NY Vol. Infantry

Born on the island of Borneo to Missionary parents. Educated at Albany Classical Institute and Rutgers College. Engaged in 14 battles of the Civil War, including Yorktown, Fairfax Court House, Turkey Bend, White Oak Swamp, Gaines Mills, Savage Station, Malvern Hill, Chantilly, Antietam, Fredericksburg and Gettysburg. Mortally wounded at Gettysburg, hit by shrapnel and shot in the arm during Pickett’s Charge.

His uncle was Rev. Henry Newman Pohlman, D.D. who presided at the dedication of Albany Rural Cemetery and was long time pastor of the 1st Lutheran Church in Albany. Lt. Pohlman is buried in his uncle’s plot.
