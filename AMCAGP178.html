Albany Medical College [Section 68, Lot 14 – 22]

Group plot for those who have donated their bodies to medical education and research

The donation of one’s body to medical research after death is a unique and priceless gift that provides a source of knowledge; the foundation of medical education and research. Advances in medical science make it necessary for physicians and biomedical scientists to conduct studies and research on humans. At Albany Medical College, the Anatomical Gift Program allows an individual who meets certain criteria, residing within 100 miles of Albany, and over 18 years of age, to donate their remains.

Every year, nearly 250 individuals enrolled in the program decease, leaving their bodies to the college. This donation allows students to research and understand human anatomy. At conclusion, all bodies are cremated and placed individually into a sealed urn. These remains may be returned to a family or loved one, or entombed during Albany Medical Center’s Annual Internment and Memorial Services.

Since 1973, this memorial service has been held annually at Albany Rural Cemetery. The service gives family and friends a sense of closure, much like a traditional funeral service. Medical students at Albany Medical College also have the opportunity to learn about the individuals life from friends and family on a more personal level.
