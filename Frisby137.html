Col. Edward Frisby (1809-1862) [Section 20 Lot 7]

25th NYS Militia; 30th NY Vol. Infantry

After a distinguished military career as commander of Albany’s 25th Regiment, Frisby retired in 1860 at the age of 51. At the outbreak of the Civil War he enlisted in the 30th NY Infantry as colonel of the regiment. Frisby was killed in action at the Second Battle of Bull Run. While leading his men in an order to “charge at double quick,” a ball struck him in the lower jaw, passing through his face. Still in the saddle, he was struck on the top of the head with an-other ball which passed through and came out the other side, killing him instantly. Four days later, his body was discovered and interred on the battlefield. Soon after, his grave was found and his body exhumed, sent to Washington for embalming, and returned to Albany for burial.
